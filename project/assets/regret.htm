Formalizing Online Learning Regret Bounds in Lean 4

We derive a rigorous upper bound on the regret of an online learner. Unlike batch optimization, we make no assumptions about the distribution of future data. Instead, we establish a foundational setup where the learner selects iterates from a convex set $\mathcal{K}$ and suffers losses from an arbitrary sequence of convex, Lipschitz functions $f_t$.

The following derivation establishes the global $O(\sqrt{T})$ rate by first analyzing the geometric stability of a single step and then accumulating these local bounds via induction.

Definition: First-Order Convexity Inequality

Claim:

$$f(y) \ge f(x) + ⟪g, y - x⟫$$

Notation:

  • $x$ : The current iterate where the subgradient is evaluated.
  • $y$ : An arbitrary point in the domain (the comparator $u$ in later proofs).
  • $g$ : The subgradient vector, acting as a linear lower-bound for the function.
  • $y - x$ : The displacement vector from the current iterate to the target.
  • $⟪\cdot, \cdot⟫$ : The standard inner product, measuring directional alignment.

Goal:

Convexity is the "engine" of optimization. This specific formulation linearizes the function, replacing a complex global gap $f(y) - f(x)$ with a simple geometric interaction. This allows Lean to work with structural inner product properties rather than arbitrary function curves. We hope to achieve a way to bound objective losses using only local vector information.

Lean 4 Code:

def convex_function_first_order_inequality (f : E → ℝ) (g : E) (x : E) : Prop :=
  ∀ y : E, f y ≥ f x + ⟪g, y - x⟫

Definition: Lipschitz Continuity Assumption

Claim:

$$\|g\| \le L$$

Notation:

  • $\|g\|$ : The magnitude or norm of the subgradient.
  • $L$ : A universal speed limit for the function's rate of change.

Goal:

Without this assumption, an adversary could choose a loss function with an infinitely steep gradient, catapulting the learner's state infinitely far away. This bounding property ensures that each discrete "jump" has a finite impact, allowing us to control the expansion of error terms.

Lean 4 Code:

def lipschitz_continuous_def (g : E) (L : ℝ) : Prop :=
  ‖g‖ ≤ L

Definition: OGD Update Rule

Claim:

$$x_{t+1} = \Pi_{\mathcal{K}}(x_t - \eta_t g_t)$$

Notation:

  • $\eta$ : The learning rate or step size.
  • $\Pi_{\mathcal{K}}$ : The projection onto the feasible convex set.

Goal:

This rule defines the learner's trajectory. By moving against the gradient and projecting back to the set, we ensure the learner's decisions remain valid while aggressively seeking the minimum. It bridges the analytical properties of $f$ and the geometric trajectory of $\{x_t\}$.

Lean 4 Code:

def ogd_update_rule_def (P : Projection E) (η : ℝ) (x g : E) : E :=
  P.proj (x - η • g)

Lemma: Squared Distance Expansion

Claim:

$$\text{dist}(x_{t+1}, u)^2 \le \text{dist}(x_t, u)^2 - 2\eta\langle g_t, x_t - u \rangle + \eta^2 \|g_t\|^2$$

Goal:

This reveals the fundamental tension in OGD: the alignment term shrinks our distance to the goal (if the gradient points "correctly"), while the noise term expands it as a penalty for the discrete step size. We hope to use this to show that the learner consistently moves closer to the optimum $u$ on average.

Lean 4 Code:

lemma ogd_step_expansion_squared_distance (P : Projection E) (η : ℝ) (x g u : E)
    (hu : ogd_projection_step_feasibility P u) :
    dist (ogd_update_rule_def P η x g) u ^ 2
      ≤ dist x u ^ 2 - 2 * η * ⟪g, x - u⟫ + η ^ 2 * ‖g‖ ^ 2 := by
  -- Apply Nonexpansiveness
  have h_nonexp := P.nonexpansive.dist_le_mul (x - η • g) u
  -- Clean up projection and constant
  rw [hu] at h_nonexp
  replace h_nonexp : dist (ogd_update_rule_def P η x g) u ≤ dist (x - η • g) u := by
    simpa using h_nonexp
  -- Square both sides
  have h_sq : dist (ogd_update_rule_def P η x g) u ^ 2 ≤ dist (x - η • g) u ^ 2 := by
    gcongr
  -- Move from 'dist' to 'norm'
  rw [dist_eq_norm, dist_eq_norm] at h_sq ⊢
  -- Expand quadratic form
  refine h_sq.trans ?_
  rw [sub_right_comm, norm_sub_sq_real]
  -- Simplify algebra
  simp only [inner_smul_right, norm_smul, Real.norm_eq_abs]
  -- η² identity
  have h_eta_sq : (|η| * ‖g‖) ^ 2 = η ^ 2 * ‖g‖ ^ 2 := by
    rw [mul_pow, sq_abs]
  rw [h_eta_sq, real_inner_comm]
  ring_nf
  apply le_refl

Lemma: Non-Expansiveness of Convex Projection

Claim:

$$\text{dist}(\Pi_{\mathcal{K}}(y), \Pi_{\mathcal{K}}(u)) \le \text{dist}(y, u)$$

Notation:

  • $\Pi_{\mathcal{K}}$ : The projection operator onto the closed convex set $\mathcal{K}$.
  • $y$ : The point in space being mapped into the set.
  • $u$ : A target point, typically the optimum.

Goal:

Projection onto a closed convex set is a contraction. This ensures that the projection step in the OGD update never pushes us further away from the optimal point $u$.

Lean 4 Code:

lemma ogd_projection_nonexpansive_property (P : Projection E) (η : ℝ) (x g u : E) :
    dist (ogd_update_rule_def P η x g) (P.proj u) ≤ dist (x - η • g) u := by
  simpa [ogd_update_rule_def]
    using (P.nonexpansive.dist_le_mul (x - η • g) u)

Lemma: Step-wise Regret Upper Bound

Claim:

$$f(x) - f(u) \le ⟪g, x - u⟫$$

Notation:

  • $f(x) - f(u)$ : The instantaneous regret at round $t$.
  • $⟪g, x - u⟫$ : The linearized surrogate for the objective gap.

Goal:

We cannot see the "optimal" loss $f(u)$ directly. This lemma shows that the alignment between the gradient and our error vector $(x-u)$ provides a surrogate measure for our loss. Bounding this inner product automatically bounds our regret.

Lean 4 Code:

lemma step_regret_convexity_upper_bound (f : E → ℝ) (g x u : E)
    (hconv : convex_function_first_order_inequality f g x) :
    f x - f u ≤ ⟪g, x - u⟫ := by
  -- Apply convexity at u
  have h := hconv u
  -- Rearrange inequality
  have h' : f x - f u ≤ -⟪g, u - x⟫ := by linarith
  -- Use inner product properties to swap arguments
  have hx : ⟪g, x - u⟫ = -⟪g, u - x⟫ := by rw [← inner_neg_right, neg_sub]
  linarith [hx]

Lemma: Inner Product to Distance Progress Relation

Claim:

$$2 * \eta * ⟪g, x - u⟫ \le \text{dist}(x, u)^2 - \text{dist}(x_{t+1}, u)^2 + \eta^2 \|g\|^2$$

Notation:

  • $\text{dist}(x, u)^2 - \text{dist}(x_{t+1}, u)^2$ : The squared distance progress toward the optimum.
  • $\eta^2 \|g\|^2$ : The step-size penalty or noise term introduced by discretization.

Goal:

Rearranging Lemma 4 to isolate the inner product term. This establishes that progress toward the optimum is a "proxy" for low instantaneous regret.

Lean 4 Code:

lemma step_regret_inner_product_to_distance_relation (P : Projection E) (η : ℝ)
    (x g u : E) (hu : ogd_projection_step_feasibility P u) :
    2 * η * ⟪g, x - u⟫
      ≤ dist x u ^ 2 - dist (ogd_update_rule_def P η x g) u ^ 2 + η ^ 2 * ‖g‖ ^ 2 := by
  -- Invoke main squared distance expansion
  have h := ogd_step_expansion_squared_distance (P := P) (η := η) (x := x) (g := g) (u := u) hu

  linarith

Lemma: Single-Step Regret Decomposition

Claim:

$$f(x) - f(u) \le \frac{\text{dist}(x, u)^2 - \text{dist}(x_{t+1}, u)^2 + \eta^2 \|g\|^2}{2\eta}$$

Notation:

  • $\frac{1}{2\eta}$ : The normalization factor required to convert distance into function value units.

Goal:

We express round $t$ regret as a sum of progress (change in distance) and noise. If we suffer high regret, we must have made significant progress toward $u$.

Lean 4 Code:

lemma step_regret_decomposition_inequality (f : E → ℝ) (g x u : E)
    (hconv : convex_function_first_order_inequality f g x)
    (P : Projection E) (η : ℝ) (hu : ogd_projection_step_feasibility P u)
    (hη_pos : 0 < η) :
    f x - f u ≤ (dist x u ^ 2 - dist (ogd_update_rule_def P η x g) u ^ 2 + η ^ 2 * ‖g‖ ^ 2) / (2 * η) := by
  -- Bound objective difference via subgradient
  have h₁ : f x - f u ≤ ⟪g, x - u⟫ := step_regret_convexity_upper_bound f g x u hconv
  -- Bound subgradient via distance progress
  have h₂ : 2 * η * ⟪g, x - u⟫ ≤ dist x u ^ 2 - dist (ogd_update_rule_def P η x g) u ^ 2 + η ^ 2 * ‖g‖ ^ 2 :=
    step_regret_inner_product_to_distance_relation P η x g u hu
  -- Divide by 2η (valid since η > 0)
  have h₄ : ⟪g, x - u⟫ ≤ (dist x u ^ 2 - dist (ogd_update_rule_def P η x g) u ^ 2 + η ^ 2 * ‖g‖ ^ 2) / (2 * η) := by
    rw [le_div_iff₀ (by linarith)]
    linarith
  -- Combine bounds
  linarith

Lemma: Optimal Step Size Balancing

Claim:

$$\frac{D^2}{2\eta} + \frac{\eta L^2 T}{2} = DL\sqrt{T} \text{ where } \eta = \frac{D}{L\sqrt{T}}$$

Notation:

  • $D$ : The diameter or maximum distance from $x_0$ to $u$.
  • $L$ : The Lipschitz constant bounding gradient norms.
  • $T$ : The time horizon or total number of steps.

Goal:

Solve for the $\eta$ that minimizes the aggregate bound $\frac{D^2}{2\eta} + \frac{\eta L^2 T}{2}$. Setting $\eta = \frac{D}{L\sqrt{T}}$ balances the initial error and cumulative noise perfectly.

Lean 4 Code:

lemma ogd_regret_optimal_step_size_choice (D L T : ℝ) (hD : 0 < D) (hL : 0 < L) (hT : 0 < T) :
    let η := D / (L * Real.sqrt T)
    D^2 / (2 * η) + η * L^2 * T / 2 = D * L * Real.sqrt T := by
  intro η
  -- Simplify root properties
  have h_sqrt : (Real.sqrt T) * (Real.sqrt T) = T := Real.mul_self_sqrt (by positivity)
  have h_L2_T : L^2 * T = (L * Real.sqrt T)^2 := by
    calc L^2 * T
      _ = L^2 * ((Real.sqrt T) * (Real.sqrt T)) := by rw [h_sqrt]
      _ = L^2 * (Real.sqrt T)^2 := by ring
      _ = (L * Real.sqrt T)^2 := by ring
  -- Positivity and non-zero invariants
  have hx_pos : 0 < L * Real.sqrt T := by positivity
  have hD_ne : D ≠ 0 := ne_of_gt hD
  have hx_ne : L * Real.sqrt T ≠ 0 := ne_of_gt hx_pos
  -- First fraction reduction
  have H1 : D^2 / (2 * (D / (L * Real.sqrt T))) = D * (L * Real.sqrt T) / 2 := by
    rw [mul_div_assoc', div_div_eq_mul_div]
    have : D^2 * (L * Real.sqrt T) / (2 * D) = D * (D * (L * Real.sqrt T)) / (D * 2) := by ring
    rw [this, mul_div_mul_left _ _ hD_ne]
  -- Second fraction reduction
  have H2 : (D / (L * Real.sqrt T)) * (L^2 * T) / 2 = D * (L * Real.sqrt T) / 2 := by
    rw [h_L2_T]
    have : (D / (L * Real.sqrt T)) * (L * Real.sqrt T)^2 / 2 = D * (L * Real.sqrt T) * (L * Real.sqrt T) / (L * Real.sqrt T) / 2 := by ring
    rw [this, mul_div_cancel_right₀ _ hx_ne]
  -- Combine
  calc D^2 / (2 * (D / (L * Real.sqrt T))) + (D / (L * Real.sqrt T)) * L^2 * T / 2
    _ = D^2 / (2 * (D / (L * Real.sqrt T))) + (D / (L * Real.sqrt T)) * (L^2 * T) / 2 := by ring
    _ = D * (L * Real.sqrt T) / 2 + D * (L * Real.sqrt T) / 2 := by rw [H1, H2]
    _ = D * L * Real.sqrt T := by ring

Theorem: The Final $O(\sqrt{T})$ Regret Bound

Claim:

$$\sum_{t=1}^T (f_t(x_t) - f_t(u)) \le DL\sqrt{T}$$

Notation:

  • $\sum (f_t(x_t) - f_t(u))$ : The cumulative regret accumulated over $T$ iterations.
  • $O(\sqrt{T})$ : The sublinear growth rate, indicating the average regret $\to 0$.

Goal:

Summing Lemma 8 over rounds. Distances telescope, leaving only initial error and noise. The average regret vanishes as $T \to \infty$. This proves OGD is a "no-regret" learner.

Lean 4 Code:

lemma ogd_regret_final_rate_theorem (T : ℕ) (f : ℕ → E → ℝ) (g x : ℕ → E) (u : E) (D L : ℝ)
    (hD : 0 < D) (hL : 0 < L) (hT : 0 < T)
    (h_dist : dist (x 0) u ≤ D)
    (h_lipschitz : ∀ t, t < T → ‖g t‖ ≤ L)
    (h_step : ∀ t, t < T → f t (x t) - f t u ≤ (dist (x t) u ^ 2 - dist (x (t + 1)) u ^ 2 + (D / (L * Real.sqrt T)) ^ 2 * ‖g t‖ ^ 2) / (2 * (D / (L * Real.sqrt T)))) :
    (∑ t ∈ Finset.range T, (f t (x t) - f t u)) ≤ D * L * Real.sqrt (T:ℝ) := by
  have hT_real : (0:ℝ) < T := by exact Nat.cast_pos.mpr hT
  set η := D / (L * Real.sqrt T)
  have h_eta_pos : 0 < η := by positivity

  -- Apply Inductive Accumulation
  have h_bound := ogd_regret_inductive_bound T f g x u η h_step

  -- Bound Subgradient Magnitudes (Lipschitz assumption over time T)
  have h_g_sum : (∑ t ∈ Finset.range T, ‖g t‖ ^ 2) ≤ (T:ℝ) * L ^ 2 := by
    calc (∑ t ∈ Finset.range T, ‖g t‖ ^ 2)
      _ ≤ ∑ t ∈ Finset.range T, L ^ 2 := by
        apply Finset.sum_le_sum
        intro t ht
        have h_t_lt : t < T := Finset.mem_range.mp ht
        have hl := h_lipschitz t h_t_lt
        gcongr
      _ = (T:ℝ) * L ^ 2 := by simp

  -- Initial Distance Condition (Domain size invariant D)
  have h_dist_sq : dist (x 0) u ^ 2 ≤ D ^ 2 := by
    have hD_pos : 0 ≤ D := by linarith
    gcongr

  -- Drop non-positive final trailing distance
  have h_dist_T_pos : 0 ≤ dist (x T) u ^ 2 := by positivity

  -- Combine parts to substitute maximum values in the inductive numerator bounds
  have h_num : dist (x 0) u ^ 2 - dist (x T) u ^ 2 + η ^ 2 * ∑ t ∈ Finset.range T, ‖g t‖ ^ 2 ≤ D ^ 2 + η ^ 2 * ((T:ℝ) * L ^ 2) := by
    have h_part1 : dist (x 0) u ^ 2 - dist (x T) u ^ 2 ≤ D ^ 2 := by linarith
    have h_part2 : η ^ 2 * ∑ t ∈ Finset.range T, ‖g t‖ ^ 2 ≤ η ^ 2 * ((T:ℝ) * L ^ 2) := by gcongr
    linarith

  have h_div : (dist (x 0) u ^ 2 - dist (x T) u ^ 2 + η ^ 2 * ∑ t ∈ Finset.range T, ‖g t‖ ^ 2) / (2 * η) ≤ (D ^ 2 + η ^ 2 * ((T:ℝ) * L ^ 2)) / (2 * η) := by
    gcongr

  -- Rearrange division fraction towards substitution of optimal step-size lemma
  have h_split : (D ^ 2 + η ^ 2 * ((T:ℝ) * L ^ 2)) / (2 * η) = D ^ 2 / (2 * η) + η * L ^ 2 * (T:ℝ) / 2 := by
    calc (D ^ 2 + η ^ 2 * ((T:ℝ) * L ^ 2)) / (2 * η)
      _ = D ^ 2 / (2 * η) + η ^ 2 * ((T:ℝ) * L ^ 2) / (2 * η) := by rw [add_div]
      _ = D ^ 2 / (2 * η) + η * L ^ 2 * (T:ℝ) / 2 := by
        congr 1
        have h_eta_ne : η ≠ 0 := ne_of_gt h_eta_pos
        calc η ^ 2 * ((T:ℝ) * L ^ 2) / (2 * η)
          _ = (η * η) * ((T:ℝ) * L ^ 2) / (η * 2) := by ring
          _ = η * (η * ((T:ℝ) * L ^ 2)) / (η * 2) := by ring
          _ = η * ((T:ℝ) * L ^ 2) / 2 := by rw [mul_div_mul_left _ _ h_eta_ne]
          _ = η * L ^ 2 * (T:ℝ) / 2 := by ring

  -- Apply Optimal Step Size identity
  have h_opt := ogd_regret_optimal_step_size_choice D L (T:ℝ) hD hL hT_real
  linarith
